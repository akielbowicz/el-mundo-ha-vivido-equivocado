## Legal Framework

### Argentina — Ley 11.723 (Régimen Legal de la Propiedad Intelectual)

**Artículo 10** — Se considera lícita la reproducción de fragmentos de obras
literarias, científicas o didácticas, siempre que:
- No superen **1.000 palabras** (o el equivalente en otros formatos)
- Se cite la **fuente** y el **autor**
- No haya **ánimo de lucro** directo
- No afecte la **explotación normal** de la obra ni cause un **perjuicio
  injustificado** a los intereses legítimos del autor

Additionally, the site's **educational/cultural purpose** (reading club, literary
commentary) strengthens the fair-dealing case under both Argentine and
international copyright norms (Berne Convention three-step test).

## Recommendations

### 1. Keep license field as-is
`"Fragmento (derecho de cita)"` is accurate and informative. It signals to
both users and authors' rights organizations that the usage is limited.

### 2. Future textos: prefer public domain
For full-text publications (not fragments), prefer works in the public domain:
- Author died before 1956 in Argentina → PD
- Works published before 1929 in the US → PD

Candidates for future PD textos: Horacio Quiroga (†1937), Leopoldo Lugones
(†1938), Alfonsina Storni (†1938), Antonio Machado (†1939).

### 3. Add an explicit copyright page
Consider a `/legal/` or `/licencias/` page listing all used works with
attributions, as a best practice for derecho de cito compliance.

### 4. Replace samples before real launch
The current samples are placeholders for development. Before public launch
(if the site gains significant traffic), replace with either:
- Original writings by the site authors, or
- Clear public-domain works, or
- Explicitly licensed fragments with written permission
